The Brands That Built the Luxury Vocabulary

Filter the maisons by aesthetic and open each card for a sharper editorial snapshot of history, identity, and hero products.

01
Parisian Classic

Chanel

Founder: Gabrielle "Coco" Chanel

Founded in 1910, Chanel changed modern womenswear by replacing restrictive fashion with ease, confidence, and understated elegance. The house became legendary through tweed tailoring, quilted leather, pearls, and a black-and-white visual language that still feels timeless.

Key Characteristics

Refined femininity, quilted textures, camellias, polished tailoring, and effortless French luxury.

Famous Items

2.55 bag, Classic Flap, tweed jackets, slingback heels, Chanel No. 5.

02
Travel Legacy

Louis Vuitton

Founder: Louis Vuitton

Established in 1854 as a trunk maker, Louis Vuitton built its identity around craftsmanship, travel, and instantly recognizable monogram luxury. Its fashion universe now spans leather goods, ready-to-wear, and runway collections that merge heritage with futuristic edge.

Key Characteristics

Monogram canvas, sculptural accessories, travel references, sharp leather goods, and modern status symbolism.

Famous Items

Speedy, Keepall, Neverfull, Capucines, monogram trunks, LV trainers.

03
Italian Drama

Gucci

Founder: Guccio Gucci

Gucci began in Florence in 1921 and evolved from fine luggage and leather goods into one of the most visible names in fashion. The brand is known for combining traditional Italian craft with bold image-making, equestrian heritage, and instantly recognizable house codes.

Key Characteristics

Horsebit hardware, web stripes, maximal styling, rich materials, and heritage with attitude.

Famous Items

Jackie bag, Bamboo 1947, Horsebit loafers, GG Marmont, Dionysus bag.

04
Intellectual Minimalism

Prada

Founder: Mario Prada

Prada was founded in 1913 in Milan and rose to modern influence by making restraint, nylon, and conceptual style feel luxurious. The brand's appeal comes from intelligent design, cooler silhouettes, and a refined tension between utility and fashion.

Key Characteristics

Clean lines, technical materials, subtle branding, cerebral styling, and elevated utility.

Famous Items

Re-Nylon bags, Galleria bag, triangle logo pieces, America's Cup sneakers, minimalist tailoring.

05
Couture Romance

Dior

Founder: Christian Dior

Since 1946, Dior has embodied Parisian refinement, first redefining postwar style with the sculptural "New Look." Today the house continues to balance couture fantasy, delicate detail, and commercial icons across fashion, beauty, and accessories.

Key Characteristics

Architectural silhouettes, florals, femininity, couture precision, and elegant embellishment.

Famous Items

Lady Dior, Saddle bag, Book Tote, Bar jacket, J'adore fragrance.

News / Trends

2024-2025 Collections and What's Popular Now

Recent runway energy keeps returning to recognizable house codes, polished accessory stories, and silhouettes that feel more exact than oversized.

Chanel

Spring-Summer 2025 moved toward lightness

Chanel's recent ready-to-wear direction leaned into fluid movement, softer structure, and a lighter expression of classic codes. The effect kept the house elegant while making it feel fresher and more relaxed.

Louis Vuitton

Cruise 2025 kept travel and sculptural dressing central

Louis Vuitton continued building around movement, destination, and artistic silhouettes, pairing travel heritage with embellished accessories and high-impact shapes that still read as luxurious and modern.

Gucci

Heritage details remain a major asset

Gucci's current fashion language keeps returning to house emblems, polished leather pieces, and stronger tailoring. That has made classic accessories and logo-linked icons feel newly relevant again.

Prada

Minimalism is getting warmer and more tactile

Prada's recent collections continue refining clean silhouettes, but with more texture, instinct, and emotional softness. It is minimal fashion that still feels directional, not cold.

Dior

Couture is embracing memory, transparency, and drama

Dior's latest couture storytelling mixed historical references with airy construction, embroidery, and theatrical shape. The house still thrives when romance is paired with strong structure.

Now Popular

Bags, sneakers, and vintage pieces are leading demand

East-west bags, heritage loafers, brown and suede sneakers, archival logo bags, and collectible top-handle styles are especially visible now. Luxury shoppers are leaning toward pieces that feel iconic, wearable, and instantly legible.
